# Tone Of Voice

This document explains how Obsura should sound in different contexts.

See also: [Verbal Identity](verbal-identity.md), [Messaging](messaging.md)

## Voice Attributes

### Clear

Use concrete wording that explains what the product does.

### Precise

Prefer the exact term over a broader synonym when the distinction matters.

### Calm

Obsura should sound composed, not alarmist or defensive.

### Trustworthy

State limits, status, and future direction honestly.

### Technical Without Being Dense

Write for users who work with screenshots, logs, configs, and systems, but do
not drown them in unnecessary jargon.

### Open-Source Professional

Sound serious and maintainable without sounding corporate or stiff.

## Do And Don't

| Do | Don't |
| --- | --- |
| Explain the workflow in plain language | Hide the product behind abstract platform talk |
| Mark future capabilities clearly | Present roadmap ideas as shipped features |
| Sound steady and competent | Sound grandiose, edgy, or defensive |
| Use task-oriented copy | Use hype-driven slogans as substitutes for meaning |

## Context Examples

### Documentation

Do:

"Obsura helps users review and remove sensitive values before they share
screenshots, logs, or technical text."

Do not:

"Obsura transforms privacy into an intelligent next-generation trust layer."

### Repo Descriptions

Do:

"Brand assets, logo exports, and reusable copy guidance for Obsura."

Do not:

"The definitive home of world-class privacy branding and next-gen secure
identity systems."

### Website Or Product Copy

Do:

"Modern redaction for screenshots, logs, and technical text."

Do not:

"AI-powered secrecy for unstoppable teams."

### Community-Facing Text

Do:

"This is the current direction. If implementation constraints change it, update
the brand guidance here first."

Do not:

"Trust us, it will all make sense later."

## Editing Rule

If a sentence sounds more dramatic than useful, rewrite it until the product and
the reader's task are both obvious.
