# UI Principles

> Status: approved product UI guidance.

This document defines how Obsura should behave visually and experientially.

See also: [Brand System Freeze](brand-system-freeze.md),
[Design Foundations](design-foundations.md),
[Interaction Principles](interaction-principles.md),
[Component Guidelines](component-guidelines.md)

## 1. Calm First Impression

Obsura should feel composed at first glance. The product should not open with a
security-theater posture, a warning-heavy dashboard, or decorative noise.

## 2. Content Is The Center

The file, screenshot, text block, or review target should remain more important
than the chrome around it.

## 3. Light By Default, Dark By Need

The brand should live on light surfaces. Dark surfaces are a task decision for
review-heavy moments, not the default identity.

## 4. One Accent Per Decision Zone

Each local area should have one clear primary action and one clear accent
language. Competing highlights reduce trust.

## 5. Review Before Apply

Obsura is not an automation theater product. Proposed changes should stay
inspectable before export or finalization.

## 6. Floating Tools Stay Contextual

Toolbars, AI helpers, menus, and popovers should appear close to the relevant
content and disappear cleanly when finished.

## 7. Dense Information Must Stay Breathable

Tables, inspectors, metadata strips, and review lists can be dense, but they
must keep enough spacing and contrast to remain calm and readable.

## 8. Semantic Color Supports, Not Leads

Status color should reinforce meaning, not carry it alone, and should never
turn the product into a red-yellow-green control room.

## 9. AI Assistance Stays Secondary

AI suggestions may speed up work, but the UI must preserve user review,
ownership, and visibility of what changed.

## 10. Important Actions Must Feel Safe

Users should feel in control when they redact, replace, confirm, or export.
Critical actions need clear framing and stable feedback.

## 11. Dangerous Actions Must Be Obvious

Destructive actions such as reset, discard, or irreversible export steps should
be visually clear and semantically distinct from ordinary actions.

## 12. Motion Confirms, It Does Not Entertain

Motion should clarify where something came from, what changed, or what gained
focus. It should not exist to make the interface feel showy.

## 13. Repeated Use Matters More Than First Demo

The product should feel better on the hundredth review task than in a single
marketing screenshot.

That means:

- frequent actions should stay close at hand
- states should be easy to understand at a glance
- repeated review cycles should not feel heavy

## 14. Confidence Comes From Visibility

Users trust the result when they can see what was detected, what changed, what
remains selected, and what will be exported.
