# Visual Direction

This document captures the approved visual character for Obsura after extracting
repeating patterns from `assets/samples_ui_ux/`.

See also: [Brand Overview](brand-overview.md),
[Brand System Freeze](brand-system-freeze.md),
[Logo Usage](logo-usage.md)

## Visual Character

Obsura should look:

- calm
- precise
- airy
- modern
- quietly premium
- structured
- high-trust
- restrained
- soft without feeling casual

## Color Direction

Obsura should lean toward:

- warm paper, mist, and white product surfaces
- dark ink text rather than heavy black framing
- one periwinkle primary accent for action and focus
- pale lavender and pale ice tones for support, selection, and gentle emphasis
- mint, amber, and coral semantic accents used sparingly

Avoid:

- black-heavy app chrome as the default identity
- neon palettes
- hacker green or threat-themed styling
- loud gradients, glows, or glassmorphism spectacle
- overuse of bright warning colors
- decorative color noise that competes with review states

## Typography Direction

Typography should feel contemporary, technical, and highly readable.

Prefer:

- a neutral grotesk sans with strong clarity at small sizes; `Geist` is the
  preferred reference family
- readable monospace support for logs, code-like text, identifiers, and
  technical surfaces; `Geist Mono` is the preferred companion
- hierarchy that favors scanning, confidence, and calm spacing
- body and metadata sizing that stays crisp inside dense layouts

Avoid typography that feels:

- playful
- luxury-branded
- retro-tech
- overly futuristic
- generic startup-soft

## Logo Concept Direction

Treat the existing mark as a compact, geometric identifier.

Default supporting contexts should be white, paper, or mist surfaces. When the
mark appears in an avatar, app tile, or compact branded container, it should
sit inside a rounded square or rounded rectangle with precise internal padding.

Future logo work should preserve that precise technical character rather than
shifting into obvious shields, padlocks, spy imagery, or "classified" stamp
motifs.

## Surface And Layout Direction

Obsura should favor:

- light app shells with white elevated working surfaces
- subtle borders and shallow ambient shadows
- floating menus, toolbars, and inspectors
- left-nav plus main-canvas plus right-inspector layouts where the workflow
  needs them
- dark surfaces only where the reviewed content benefits from contrast

## UI Alignment

Product surfaces should support careful work:

- clear hierarchy
- readable scan paths
- one obvious primary action per local context
- deliberate use of color
- clean annotation and review surfaces
- minimal decorative weight around sensitive content
- contextual assistants that stay secondary to review and confirmation

The interface should help users notice, decide, review, and export.

## Status

This direction is frozen as the approved brand reference until deliberately
changed in this repository.
